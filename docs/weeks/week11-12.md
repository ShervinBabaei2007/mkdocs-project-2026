# High Fidelity Wireframes

This week marked a major milestone in my redesign process. After analyzing the original site’s usability flaws, I moved into high-fidelity wireframing with a focus on three specific Nielsen Norman Heuristics.

## The Problem vs. The Solution

The original site suffered from extreme "visual noise"—think zigzag backgrounds and skull icons on every link. My redesign strips this back to the essentials:

- **Typography:** I paired **Raleway** (for headers) with **Inter** (for body text). This combination balances unique brand character with high-performance screen legibility.

- **Color Palette:** I moved to a functional 3-color system:
  - **Deep Navy** for stability
  - **Light Blue** for secondary layout elements
  - **Orange** for buttons
  This ensures users always know exactly where to click.

## Core Focus Areas

- **Heuristic #1 (Aesthetics):** Minimalist styling of product cards to highlight the actual items.

- **Heuristic #4 (Consistency):** Standardized navigation so the site feels intuitive.

- **Heuristic #6 (Recognition):** On-screen filters and sidebars help users find what they need without memorizing the site map.
